Our Story

38 Years in the Making

It started with hand-written cheat sheets, a pencil, and the Sunday newspaper. Before the internet, before apps, before algorithms — fantasy football was a game of paper, patience, and passion. Did you win your matchup this week? You'd find out when the results arrived by mail.

Through nearly four decades of fantasy football — from those hand-written rosters to spreadsheets to the digital age — the obsession never faded. If anything, it grew. The game evolved. The data exploded. But something was always missing.

"I built this with my son. Two generations of fantasy football passion, combined with the most powerful AI technology ever created. This is The Inner Sanctum."

We created The Inner Sanctum because every other tool felt the same — cold data, no personality, no wisdom. We wanted something different. Something that felt like getting advice from your most knowledgeable friend, your funniest rival, and your sharpest analyst — all at once.

The Evolution of Fantasy Football
1980s Hand-written cheat sheets. Results delivered by postal mail. The original grinders.
1990s Fax machines and phone hotlines. ESPN enters the game. Fantasy goes mainstream.
2000s The internet revolution. Yahoo and ESPN launch free platforms. 15 million players.
2010s Mobile apps, DFS, Sleeper. Real-time data, live scoring. 60 million players.
2025 The Inner Sanctum. AI-powered. Three personas. Built by a father and son.
