Draft Strategy

🏃 RB Heavy
Load up on elite running backs. Spend big on two RB1s and fill the rest cheap.
Best for: PPR leagues with strong RB ADP
🎯 WR Heavy
Target elite receivers. Let others overpay for RBs while you secure top pass catchers.

Best for: Superflex / 2QB leagues

What These Numbers Mean
📌Each dollar amount is your maximum recommended spend for that position slot.
⚖️The numbers are calculated so your total always equals your exact budget — not a penny over.
📊They reflect where the market should price players — not where it will. The draft decides that.
"Your Target Price Sheet is a guardrail — not a rulebook. It is your starting position, not your finish line."
The Most Important Rule of Auction Drafts
The auction has a rhythm. Your job is to read the room and pivot.
How to Use Your Sheet During the Draft
💰If a player goes cheap — bank the savings and spend more on the next position.
🔥If a player goes expensive — protect your remaining budget, let rivals overpay.
📈Watch inflation — when the room overpays early, remaining values shift upward.
🔄Switch sheets when the room tells you to — that's not weakness, that's winning.
❤️Never fall in love with a number — the sheet guides you, the draft decides.
⭐ Pro Strategy — Print Multiple Par Sheets
The smartest auction drafters walk into the room with 2-3 target price sheets — one for each strategy. Generate a sheet for your primary strategy, a pivot strategy, and a backup. If elite RBs go cheap early, switch to your RB Heavy sheet instantly. If QBs are flying off the board, your QB Heavy sheet is ready. The manager with one sheet is reactive. The manager with three sheets is always in control.
When to Pivot Strategies
RBs going cheap?Switch to RB Heavy — load up on elite backs while rivals sleep.
WRs going cheap?Switch to WR Heavy — receivers at a discount are championship currency.
Everyone overpaying early?Go conservative — your budget sheet becomes your biggest weapon.
QBs flying off board?Switch to QB Heavy — or stream and feast on value elsewhere.
